How to Network at UMSI

Where to Get Started

Here are a few ways to start building your connections today.

  1. Reach out to someone you already know.
    Talk to a classmate, professor, or student organization leader about their experiences.
  2. Set up one informational interview:
    Use the University Career Alumni Network (UCAN) or LinkedIn to connect with an alum and ask about their career journey.
  3. Attend a networking event.
    Check the UMSI Career Development Office calendar for upcoming workshops and fairs where you can practice your skills.

Why Networking Matters

Networking is one of the most powerful ways to learn about career opportunities, gain advice from experienced professionals, and build connections that can lead to internships or jobs. At UMSI, you have access to alumni, peers, faculty, and industry experts who are eager to share their insights. By starting early and practicing your networking skills, you’ll feel more confident approaching new opportunities.

Tips for Successful Networking

  • Start Small: Reach out to classmates, faculty, or alumni from student groups before connecting with new contacts.
  • Be Curious: Ask thoughtful questions about someone’s career path, role, or organization rather than focusing only on job openings.
  • Follow Up: Always send a thank-you message after a conversation to express appreciation and keep the connection alive.
  • Practice Your Pitch: A short “elevator pitch” about your interests and skills will help people remember you.
  • Stay Organized: Track your contacts, conversations, and next steps in a document or spreadsheet.

Networking Opportunities at UMSI

The School of Information provides many ways for students to connect with professionals and peers. Take advantage of events and platforms designed to support your career journey:

  • Career Fairs: Meet recruiters from a wide range of industries each semester.
  • Workshops: Practice your networking skills in a supportive environment with CDO staff.
  • UCAN: Use UCAN to connect directly with Michigan alumni.
  • Student Organizations: Join UMSI groups and clubs to connect with peers who share your interests.
