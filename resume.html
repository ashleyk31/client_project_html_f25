Where to Get Started

Getting started on your resume can be overwhelming, but the key is to begin with the basics.

  1. Start ny choosing a template.
    Then gather your experiences, such as class projects, internships, and volunteer work, that best highlight your skills
  2. Use action-oriented language.
    Describe your impact with strong verbs and specific results.
  3. Check your progress.
    Use the resume resources linked below to learn more about effective writing and make sure you have everything necessary included.

Showcasing Your Skills and Experiences

Beyond listing your job titles and responsibilities, your resume should highlight the unique skills and experiences that set you apart. Think about the tools, technologies, and methods you’ve learned at UMSI and how they apply to real-world problems. Employers want to see not only what you’ve done, but also how you think and contribute.

Ideas for What to Include

  • Technical Skills: Programming languages, data analysis tools, design software, or UX research methods you’ve mastered.
  • Projects: Capstone, client, or class projects that demonstrate collaboration, leadership, or problem-solving.
  • Community Engagement: Volunteer work, student organizations, or leadership experiences that show initiative and impact.
  • Achievements: Scholarships, awards, or recognition from faculty, peers, or employers.
